Books

Learn to Code HTML & CSS
 by Shay Howe
BUY HERE

I highly recommend this book to a newbie or even a seasoned developer who has forgotten the basics of symantec HTML5 & CSS3 and needs a refresher. NF. 5 of 5 stars

CCS Foundations
 by Ian Lunn
BUY HERE

Again a very good book from Team Treehouse. Covers all one needs to know about CSS3 initially and a 'bit more'. A lot of the 'bit more' can be implemented easier today with jQuery. NF. 4.5 of 5 stars

Build Your Own Website The Right Way Using HTML & CSS
 by Ian Lloyd
BUY HERE

A useful book and easy to follow. Good recommendations for add-ons to include in your library. All you need to know to get an effective website up and running. NF. 4 of 5 stars

Creating a Web Site: The Missing Manual: The Missing Manual
 by Matthew MacDonald
BUY HERE

Filled a few holes in my overall web knowledge and will get you up and running fast. But, the book is now becoming a bit dated and could do with a makeover. NF. 3 of 5 stars

Head First HTML and CSS
 by Elisabeth Robson
BUY HERE

I like the crazy format. Not the norm, but works extremely well. A book not to be missed if you don't like reams of data thrown at you. NF. 4.5 of 5 stars

HTML5 Foundations
 by Matt West
BUY HERE

As expected from Team Treehouse, a very good book. Good structure and links to free Treehouse videos also. Should be named though, HTML and Javascript! NF. 5 of 5 stars
